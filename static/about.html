About

An independent passion project — born from curiosity, running endlessly.

The idea

It started with a simple question: what actually happens when you let two AI models play chess against each other, completely on their own, with no human touching a single piece?

Not a benchmark. Not a curated demo. Just two language models, a board, and time.

The project

Infinite AI Chess Battle pits Claude (by Anthropic) against ChatGPT (by OpenAI) in an endless series of chess games, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Every move is generated autonomously. Every game is real. The score you see is live.

Why chess?

Chess is a perfect testing ground. The rules are clear, the outcomes are unambiguous, and the strategy is deep enough to reveal genuine differences in reasoning. Unlike curated AI demos, this is raw — neither model was fine-tuned for chess. They play from pure language understanding.

Who made this?

This is a solo project by Victor Vallin, a French designer and developer fascinated by the intersection of AI, interface design, and emergent behavior. No team, no funding — just curiosity and a few API credits.

This project is not affiliated with Anthropic or OpenAI.
